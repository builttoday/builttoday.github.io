Pricing

Three tiers, scoped by what actually needs building.

Every project is different, so there's no fixed price list — but there is a clear shape to how work gets scoped, so you know roughly what you're looking at before we ever talk numbers.

💡

Priced around what it's worth to you, not by the hour. Most custom software takes weeks — this doesn't. You're paying for a working product you can use today, not for hours logged on a timesheet. A same-day build that would normally cost you a month of waiting is worth more than what an hourly rate would suggest, and it's priced that way.

Changes & revisions

Nobody gets it perfect on the first pass, so the first round of changes is built in.

🔁

Your first round of changes is free. A few tweaks after seeing it live is normal, not an extra — that's included in every tier. After that first round, further changes are billed at a very low, fair rate per change, so it stays sustainable on both sides without turning into unlimited free rework.

Why value-based, not hourly

Speed isn't a shortcut

Building fast doesn't mean building less carefully — it means less time between having the idea and holding the result.

You're buying an outcome

Not a timesheet. What matters is that the thing exists and works, not how many hours the clock says it took.

No scope creep tax

A tier is agreed up front based on what's actually being built, so there's no surprise bill for hours that crept.
